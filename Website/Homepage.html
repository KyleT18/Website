Brief History

The Miami Heat is an American professional basketball team based in Miami, Florida.
The team was launched in 1988 and played in the 1988–89 season of the National Basketball Association.
The next season they moved from the Western Conference to the Atlantic Division of the Eastern Conference.
After a mainly mediocre performance in the first few seasons, there were extensive team changes in 1995 and 1996 under head coach Pat Riley.
They reached the Eastern Conference Finals for the first time in 1996-97. They did not reach this level again until 2004-05, under new head coach Stan Van Gundy and with Dwyane Wade.
At this time they transferred to the Southeast Division. The next year the team won the NBA Finals, defeating the Dallas Mavericks.
The head coach position was next taken by Erik Spoelstra. Their next successful season was 2010-11, when they reached the NBA Finals again, losing to the Mavericks. After acquiring LeBron James and Chris Bosh to team with Dwyane Wade, they won the Finals in both 2012 and 2013, and won 27 successive games in February-March 2013. In 2013-14 they reached the Finals again but lost to San Antonio Spurs.
Lebron James left the team in July 2014, and the 2014-15 season was less successful, with the team missing the playoffs.
